Bonjour!

My name is Achille, I am French, and throughout my career, I have been committed to developing interpretable AI solutions for real-world problems. Precisely, I aim to build tools that are directly applicable by domain experts, tailored to their practical needs. In particular, I believe that meaningful innovation emerges from interdisciplinary collaboration. New discoveries and ideas hide in the interstices between expert communities and bridging them is essential! Finally, interpretable AI is essential for breaking down technical silos and empowering users with understanding and trust.

Experience

  1. Consultant

    Visual Meaning (Start-up) February 2024 – Present
    Bridging the gap between developers and non-technical consultants, I design and prototype software solutions to transform complex corporate data into accessible knowledge graphs that underlie interactive visual maps.

  2. Postdoctoral Researcher

    University of Oxford, IBME May 2022 – January 2024
    In collaboration with surgeons, I have developed a Python prototype of an interpretable clinical decision support system to support the decision of accepting or not a kidney transplant with interpretable ML.

  3. PhD Researcher

    Nokia Bell Labs October 2017 – July 2021
    During this industrial PhD (CIFRE), I developed pattern matching solutions for alarm prediction and root cause analysis in telecommunication networks. I have delivered a Python root-cause analysis module, which has been transferred to Nokia’s business units. Alternatively, I have produced a theoretical cartography of Hidden Markov Models and Recurrent Neural Networks’ expressivities.

  4. Lab Supervisor

    September 2018 – November 2020
    For two consecutive years, I supervised lab sessions for a course on Scientific Calculus (Master 1) and another one on Image Segmentation with Hidden Markov Models (Master 2) at Télécom SudParis. These courses were respectively coordinated by Yohan Petetin, and Wojciech Pieczynski and Emmanuel Monfrini.
